What Is MBTI?

MBTI (Myers-Briggs Type Indicator) is a personality model that helps
understand how people perceive the world and make decisions.
MBTI is not designed to put people into boxes; rather, it is a system used to understand our behavioral preferences.

Primary Objectives of MBTI:
  • To help you discover and understand yourself
  • To comprehend social relationships better
  • To identify your learning and working styles

How Is MBTI Determined?

An MBTI personality type is not just a simple four-letter label; behind every type lies a specific cognitive function stack. The correct way to understand an MBTI type is to examine how these functions operate. There are 8 functions in total.

The Structure of Each Type
  • Dominant Function: The core operating mode of the personality.
  • Auxiliary Function: The aspect that supports the dominant function.
  • Tertiary Function: The weaker aspect that develops over time.
  • Inferior Function: The least developed side, which typically manifests under stress.

Perception Functions (How do you gather information?)

• Se (Extraverted Sensing): Focusing on the present moment, physical senses, and real-time experiences.

• Si (Introverted Sensing): Relying on past experiences, memory, and established habits.

• Ne (Extraverted Intuition): Seeing possibilities, alternative paths, and new ideas.

• Ni (Introverted Intuition): Capturing deep meanings, patterns, and internal insights oriented toward the future.

Judgment Functions (How do you make decisions?)

• Te (Extraverted Thinking): Decision-making based on order, efficiency, and objective results.

• Ti (Introverted Thinking): Focusing on internal accuracy, system building, and logical consistency.

• Fe (Extraverted Feeling): Considering people's needs, social harmony, and relational balance.

• Fi (Introverted Feeling): Making decisions based on personal values, internal truth, and authenticity.
